Bridal Beauty Timeline Before the Big Day

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

Glowing wedding-day skin and perfect brows don't happen overnight. Here's when to book each treatment so everything peaks on the day — not before.

3 months before

Start monthly facials to build clear, even skin, and do your eyebrow embroidery now — this leaves time to heal and complete the included touch-up. New to PMU? Read how to prepare.

1 month before

Keep up facials and book any lip blush if you want effortless wedding lips. Avoid trying brand-new aggressive treatments this close.

1 week before

  • Final hydrating facial (no heavy extraction this close) for a glow.
  • Fresh set of eyelash extensions — book 2–5 days out so they settle.

2–3 days before

Your gel manicure (and pedicure) — gel stays flawless for the wedding and honeymoon.

Day before

Rest, hydrate, and avoid anything new on your skin. You've already done the work — now glow.

Tip: book early. Bridal slots fill fast, and PMU especially needs healing time. Message us to plan your timeline.

FAQ

Common questions

When should a bride do eyebrow embroidery?

About 2–3 months before the wedding, so the brows fully heal and you complete the included touch-up in time.

When should I get lash extensions before my wedding?

Book a fresh set 2–5 days before, so they settle and look their best on the day.
