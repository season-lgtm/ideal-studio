Maskne: Why It Happens & How to Treat It

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

Breakouts around the mouth, chin and cheeks from wearing masks? That's "maskne" — and Singapore's heat makes it worse.

Why it happens

Masks trap heat, humidity and sweat against the skin and create friction, which clogs pores and irritates the barrier — the perfect recipe for breakouts.

How to prevent it

  • Use a clean, breathable mask and change it when damp.
  • Skip heavy makeup under the mask.
  • Cleanse after wearing to remove sweat and oil — especially in humidity. See our humidity routine.
  • Keep the barrier strong with a light moisturiser; don't over-strip.

How to treat it

  • Spot-treat with salicylic acid or benzoyl peroxide — see acne ingredients.
  • Don't pick.
  • Book a deep cleansing facial to clear congestion and calm inflammation.

With consistent care it clears quickly — book a facial if it's stubborn.

FAQ

Common questions

What causes maskne?

Trapped heat, humidity, sweat and friction from masks clog pores and irritate skin, especially in hot, humid weather.

How do I get rid of mask acne?

Cleanse after wearing, use breathable masks, spot-treat with salicylic acid, avoid picking, and get a deep cleansing facial for stubborn congestion.
