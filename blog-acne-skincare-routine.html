A Simple Skincare Routine for Acne-Prone Skin

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

Clear skin doesn't need 10 steps. A short, consistent routine beats a complicated one you won't keep up.

Morning

  • Gentle cleanser — removes overnight oil without stripping.
  • Niacinamide serum — controls oil and calms redness.
  • Light moisturiser — gel or lotion, non-comedogenic.
  • SPF 30–50 — every single day. Protects and prevents marks.

Night

  • Cleanse (double cleanse if you wore sunscreen/makeup).
  • Treatment — salicylic acid (BHA) a few nights a week, or a retinoid if your skin tolerates it. Don't layer everything at once. See acne ingredients explained.
  • Moisturise — yes, even oily skin needs it.

What to skip

Harsh scrubs, alcohol-heavy toners, and piling on actives — they damage your barrier and worsen acne. Less is more.

Add professional support

Home care maintains; a deep cleansing facial resets. Monthly extraction keeps pores clear — more in the best facial for acne.

FAQ

Common questions

How many skincare steps do I need for acne?

Four is plenty: cleanser, a treatment ingredient, moisturiser and SPF. Consistency matters far more than the number of steps.

Should oily, acne-prone skin use moisturiser?

Yes. Skipping moisturiser makes skin produce more oil. Use a light, non-comedogenic gel or lotion.
