Adult Hormonal Acne: Causes & Management

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

Breaking out in your 20s, 30s or beyond? You're not alone. Hormonal acne is extremely common in adults — and very manageable once you understand it.

How to spot it

Hormonal acne tends to appear on the lower face — jawline, chin and neck, often as deeper, tender bumps that flare on a monthly cycle or with stress.

Common triggers

  • Hormonal fluctuations (cycle, stress, lifestyle changes)
  • Stress and poor sleep raising cortisol
  • Heavy, pore-clogging products and dairy/high-sugar spikes for some people
  • Over-washing, which strips the barrier and worsens oil

How to manage it

  • Consistent gentle routine — cleanse, treat with salicylic acid/niacinamide, moisturise, SPF. See our acne-prone skincare routine.
  • Regular professional facials — a deep cleansing facial with extraction and LED keeps congestion and inflammation down between flares.
  • Don't pick — hormonal bumps scar easily.
  • See a doctor for persistent or cystic acne — topical care and facials work best alongside medical advice.

Common questions

Where does hormonal acne usually appear?

Most often on the jawline, chin and neck, as deeper, tender breakouts that flare cyclically or with stress.

Can facials help hormonal acne?

Yes — regular deep cleansing facials with extraction and LED therapy reduce congestion and inflammation, though stubborn cases also need medical advice.
