Dehydrated vs Dry Skin: How to Tell & Fix It

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

They sound the same but they're not. Dry is a skin type (lacks oil); dehydrated is a condition (lacks water) — and even oily skin can be dehydrated.

How to tell

  • Dry skin: flaky, rough, tight all over; rarely oily. A long-term type.
  • Dehydrated skin: feels tight but can still look oily, dull, with fine "crepey" lines and oily T-zone but parched cheeks. A temporary condition.

Why it's common in Singapore

Heat, sweat, air-con and over-cleansing strip water from the skin, so many people here are oily and dehydrated at once. See our humidity skincare routine.

How to fix it

  • Dehydrated: add water-binding ingredients (hyaluronic acid, glycerin), use a gentle cleanser, don't over-exfoliate, and try a hydra resurfacing facial for a fast rebound.
  • Dry: richer moisturiser with ceramides, and avoid stripping foaming cleansers.

A facial with a skin scan can tell you exactly which you're dealing with.

FAQ

Common questions

Can oily skin be dehydrated?

Yes — dehydration is a lack of water, not oil. Oily, acne-prone skin in humid climates is often dehydrated from over-cleansing and air-con.

What's the quickest fix for dehydrated skin?

Hydrating ingredients like hyaluronic acid, a gentler routine, and a hydra resurfacing facial for an immediate boost.
