How to Make Your Gel Manicure Last Longer

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

A gel manicure should last 2–3 weeks without chipping. If yours lifts early, it's almost always down to prep and home care.

It starts with prep

Proper prep — pushing back cuticles, lightly buffing, dehydrating the nail and capping the free edge with gel — is what stops lifting. This is exactly how we apply every gel manicure.

The first few hours

Even cured gel keeps settling, so avoid long hot showers, washing up and oils for the first couple of hours.

Daily habits that protect gel

  • Wear gloves for cleaning and dishwashing.
  • Apply cuticle oil daily — flexible nails chip less.
  • Don't use nails as tools (opening cans, peeling stickers).
  • Never peel or pick gel off — it takes your natural nail layer with it.

Know when to refill

Book a fresh set or removal around the 2–3 week mark before growth causes lifting. For weak nails, ask about a gel overlay for extra strength.

FAQ

Common questions

Why does my gel manicure keep chipping?

Usually under-prep or peeling it off. Proper cuticle prep, capping the free edge and daily cuticle oil dramatically improve wear.

How long should a gel manicure last?

With good prep and care, 2–3 weeks chip-free is normal.
