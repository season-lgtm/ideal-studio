Why Your Acne Keeps Coming Back

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

Cleared up, then broke out again? Recurring acne almost always traces back to a few fixable habits — not bad luck.

The usual culprits

  • Stopping treatment too soon. Skin clears, you quit the routine, congestion rebuilds. Acne care is ongoing maintenance.
  • Picking and squeezing. It spreads bacteria and re-triggers breakouts in the same spots.
  • Pore-clogging products. Heavy creams, some makeup and even hair products along the hairline.
  • Under-cleansing sunscreen/sweat in Singapore's humidity — see our humidity skincare routine.
  • Never clearing deep congestion. Blackheads and closed comedones under the surface keep erupting.

How to break the cycle

  • Keep a consistent simple routine even when skin is clear.
  • Get regular professional extractions — a deep cleansing facial clears congestion you can't reach safely at home.
  • Space facials to your skin's needs — read how often to get a facial.
  • Protect and don't pick.

FAQ

Common questions

Why does my acne keep coming back after it clears?

Usually because the routine stops once skin clears, or deep congestion was never fully treated. Consistent care plus regular facials prevent the rebound.

Does popping pimples make acne worse?

Yes — it spreads bacteria, delays healing and causes breakouts and scars in the same area.
