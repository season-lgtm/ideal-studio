Gel Overlay vs Extensions: A Simple Guide

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

Both use gel, but they solve different problems. Here's how to choose for your nails.

Gel overlay

A layer of gel applied over your natural nail to add strength and a smooth, glossy finish — without adding length. Ideal if your nails are thin, peeling or breaking. (Soft / Hard Gel Overlay +$10/$20 per set)

Gel extensions

Length is added using tips or forms, then sculpted with gel — perfect when you want longer nails or a specific shape. (Gel Extension +$25, Half Tip +$35 per set)

Which should I choose?

  • Want strength, keep your length: overlay.
  • Want extra length or shape: extensions.
  • Nails very weak right now: overlay first, extensions later.

Nail health

Both are safe when applied and removed properly. We always soak off gently rather than peel, to protect your natural nail. To keep either looking fresh, follow our make-it-last guide.

FAQ

Common questions

Is a gel overlay better for nail health than extensions?

An overlay adds strength without length and is gentler if your nails are weak. Extensions are safe too when applied and removed properly.

Do gel extensions ruin your nails?

Not if applied and soaked off correctly. Damage usually comes from peeling them off, not from the gel itself.
