Facials for Sensitive Skin: What to Look For

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

Sensitive skin doesn't mean no facials — it means the right facial. Done gently, a facial can calm and strengthen reactive skin.

What a sensitive-skin facial should include

  • A consultation and patch consideration before anything active.
  • Gentle cleansing and minimal, careful extraction — no aggressive scrubbing.
  • Soothing, barrier-supporting masks and calming steps like LED.
  • Fragrance-light, low-irritation products.

What to avoid

Harsh exfoliation, strong peels without build-up, over-extraction and heat for very reactive skin. If a treatment stings beyond mild tingling, it's too much.

How to prep

Pause strong actives (retinol, acids) a few days before, arrive with clean skin, and tell your therapist about allergies and reactions. See our before & after a facial guide.

FAQ

Common questions

Can sensitive skin get facials?

Yes, with a gentle approach — careful cleansing, minimal extraction, soothing masks and calming LED. Avoid harsh scrubs and strong peels.

How do I prepare sensitive skin for a facial?

Pause retinol and acids a few days before, arrive with clean skin, and tell your therapist about any allergies or past reactions.
