Eyebrow Embroidery Aftercare: Do's & Don'ts

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

Great eyebrow embroidery is 50% technique and 50% aftercare. Follow these and your brows heal crisp and last longer.

Do

  • Keep brows clean and dry for the first few days.
  • Apply the aftercare balm as directed — thin layers only.
  • Let any flaking shed naturally.
  • Wear SPF once healed — sun fades pigment fastest.
  • Book your touch-up at 4–6 weeks (included).

Don't

  • Don't pick or scratch scabs — it pulls out pigment and can scar.
  • Avoid sweat, saunas, swimming and steam while healing.
  • No makeup or actives (retinol, acids) on the brows during healing.
  • Avoid direct sun and tanning.

What's normal

Brows look bold for a few days, then lighten and may look patchy before the colour resurfaces — totally normal. See the full healing timeline, and how to prepare beforehand.

FAQ

Common questions

How do I care for eyebrows after embroidery?

Keep them clean and dry, apply thin aftercare balm, don't pick flaking, avoid sweat/sun/makeup while healing, and book the 4–6 week touch-up.

How long does eyebrow embroidery take to heal?

About 1–2 weeks to surface-heal, with colour settling by 4–6 weeks — when the included touch-up perfects it.
