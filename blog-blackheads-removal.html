How to Get Rid of Blackheads Safely

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

Blackheads are clogged pores that oxidise and darken — not dirt. Scrubbing harder won't shift them, and squeezing at home usually makes things worse.

Why squeezing backfires

Picking at blackheads damages the pore wall, spreads bacteria and can cause scarring or larger-looking pores. It feels productive, but it sets your skin back.

The professional way

In a deep cleansing facial, we soften pores with steam and exfoliation first, then extract gently and safely, and finish with high-frequency to calm the skin. It's the difference between clearing a pore and traumatising it. See the best facial for congested, acne-prone skin.

At home

  • BHA (salicylic acid) dissolves oil inside the pore — the single most useful blackhead ingredient.
  • Clay masks 1–2x a week absorb excess oil.
  • Retinoids (if your skin tolerates them) speed up cell turnover.
  • Always SPF — sun makes pores look worse over time.

Prevention

Consistent cleansing, weekly exfoliation and regular professional extractions keep blackheads from rebuilding. Skip pore strips — they only grab the surface and can irritate skin.

FAQ

Common questions

Is it bad to squeeze blackheads?

Yes — at-home squeezing damages the pore, spreads bacteria and can scar. Professional extraction after steam is far safer.

What ingredient is best for blackheads?

Salicylic acid (BHA) is the most effective, because it's oil-soluble and clears congestion inside the pore.
