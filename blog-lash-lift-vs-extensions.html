Lash Lift vs Extensions: Which Is Better?

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

Both open up your eyes — but one enhances your own lashes while the other adds new ones. Here's the difference.

Keratin lash lift

A lift curls and sets your natural lashes upward, making them look longer and more open — no extensions added. Low-maintenance, lasts 6–8 weeks, and great if you have decent natural length. (Keratin Lash Lift — $55)

Lash extensions

Individual extensions add length and volume for a fuller look, from natural classic to dramatic volume. More impact, but needs refills every 2–3 weeks.

Quick comparison

  • Lowest maintenance: lash lift.
  • Most length & volume: extensions.
  • Natural lashes already long: lift works beautifully.
  • Sparse or short lashes: extensions give more.

Cost & upkeep

A lift is a one-off every 6–8 weeks; extensions cost a little more over time with refills. If you love a wash-and-go routine, choose the lift; if you want maximum glam, choose extensions. Either way, follow our aftercare tips.

FAQ

Common questions

Is a lash lift better than extensions?

Neither is better — a lift is lower maintenance and enhances your own lashes; extensions add the most length and volume.

How long does a lash lift last?

Usually 6–8 weeks, following your natural lash growth cycle.
