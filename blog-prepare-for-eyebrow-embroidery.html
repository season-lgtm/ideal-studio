How to Prepare for Eyebrow Embroidery

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

A little prep makes your eyebrow embroidery heal better and last longer. Here's your pre-appointment checklist.

A few days before

  • Avoid blood thinners where possible — alcohol, fish oil and (with your doctor's OK) aspirin can cause more bleeding and weaker retention.
  • Don't wax, tint, thread or pluck your brows — we want to see your natural hair to map the shape.
  • Pause retinol & strong actives around the brow area for about a week.
  • Avoid sunburn and tanning on the forehead.

The day of

  • Skip caffeine and alcohol — they increase sensitivity and bleeding.
  • Come with clean skin and, if you like, a photo of brows you love.
  • Eat beforehand so you're comfortable during the session.

What we'll do

We start with a consultation and brow mapping to suit your face, apply numbing cream, then work in gentle passes with single-use sterile needles. Curious about comfort? Read is eyebrow embroidery painful, and the healing timeline for what comes next.

FAQ

Common questions

What should I avoid before eyebrow embroidery?

Avoid alcohol, caffeine, fish oil and other blood thinners, and don't wax, tint or pluck your brows beforehand.

Should I shape my brows before the appointment?

No — leave them natural so we can map the most flattering shape from your real brow hair.
