How Long Does Eyebrow Embroidery Last?

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

Eyebrow embroidery typically lasts 1–3 years. How long depends on your skin type, lifestyle and — most of all — aftercare during healing.

The healing timeline

  • Days 1–3: brows look bold and slightly darker. Keep them clean and dry.
  • Days 4–7: light flaking or scabbing — do not pick. Colour looks patchy temporarily.
  • Days 7–14: colour "disappears" then resurfaces softer as skin renews.
  • Week 4–6: true colour settles — time for your touch-up.

Why the touch-up matters

Skin heals unevenly, so a touch-up perfects colour and fills any gaps. Your price includes one touch-up within 4–6 weeks.

Make it last longer

  • Keep brows dry for the first few days; follow the aftercare balm routine.
  • Avoid sun, saunas, swimming and sweaty workouts while healing.
  • Wear SPF afterwards — sun fades pigment fastest.
  • Book a colour refresh every 12–18 months to stay crisp.

Oily skin tends to fade faster, which is why powder or combination brows can be a better choice for longevity.

FAQ

Common questions

How long does eyebrow embroidery last?

Usually 1–3 years depending on skin type and aftercare, with a colour refresh recommended every 12–18 months.

Why do my brows look darker right after?

Freshly done brows always look bolder. The colour softens by about 30–50% as the skin heals over 1–2 weeks.
