Brow Mapping: The Right Brows for Your Face Shape

By IDEAL STUDIO · Singapore · Updated 2026-06-25

The secret to natural-looking eyebrow embroidery isn't the needle — it's the mapping done before we start.

What brow mapping is

Using measurements from your own features — the bridge of the nose, the eye, the brow bone — we map where each brow should start, arch and end, so both brows are balanced and suit your face. Only then do we choose technique and colour.

Brows by face shape (general guide)

  • Round face: a defined arch adds length and structure.
  • Long/oval face: flatter, more horizontal brows balance length.
  • Square face: a soft curved arch softens strong angles.
  • Heart face: a gentle, rounded brow balances a narrower chin.

Technique still matters

Once mapped, we match the right technique — natural hair strokes, soft powder, or combination. See microblading vs powder vs combination to understand the finish, then book a consult and we'll map yours.

FAQ

Common questions

What is brow mapping?

Measuring your features to design where each brow should start, arch and end — so both brows are symmetrical and flatter your face before any work begins.

Will eyebrow embroidery suit my face shape?

Yes — brow mapping tailors the shape to your face (round, long, square, heart), and the technique and colour are chosen to match.
